# Zettelkasten, minus the ideology

A plain note about note-taking, deliberately without frontmatter — the
plane must index it anyway, keyed by its path.

The useful residue of the zettelkasten literature is small: write
notes in your own words, one idea per note, link generously, and trust
search over hierarchy. Everything else — numbering schemes, index
cards, the cult of the slip-box — is costume.

Links are the part machines can help with: a note you wrote in May and
a clip you saved in June are related whether or not you remembered to
connect them. That is a retrieval problem, not a discipline problem.
