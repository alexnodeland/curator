# Tomato companion planting notes

This season's bed layout and what the folklore actually earned:

- **Basil** under the tomatoes: no measurable pest effect I can see,
  but the shading keeps the soil evenly moist and the pesto keeps the
  cook happy. Stays.
- **Marigolds** at the border: the nematode claim is real but only for
  specific varieties tilled in as a cover crop — as a border flower
  they are decoration. Pretty decoration. Stays.
- **Fennel**: allelopathic, stunts nearly everything near it. The one
  piece of companion-planting folklore with teeth. Banished to a pot.

Watering discipline matters more than any companion: deep, infrequent,
at the base, mulched. Split fruit is a watering rhythm problem, not a
variety problem.
