# Sourdough hydration, in practice

Another deliberately plain note (no frontmatter) — path identity again.

Percentages are baker's math: water as a share of flour weight. 70%
is the honest starting point for a home oven — slack enough for an
open crumb, tight enough to shape without despair. Push toward 80%
only after shaping at 70% feels boring.

What actually moved the needle here was not hydration at all: a hotter
oven (as high as it goes, steel preheated 45 minutes) and a longer,
colder proof (overnight in the fridge) improved the loaf more than any
water adjustment ever did.
